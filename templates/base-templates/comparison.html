Comparison of Statistics

Statistic TypeComparisonInsight
Pitching Batting Average Allowed and Runs Allowed Pitchers that allow a batting average of 0.245 or higher typically allow a higher number of runs per the number of hits allowed (75 innings played or more). For these pitchers, their runs allowed to hits allowed percentage is 40% or higher. This means that pitchers that allow a higher batting avergae will typically have a harder time with preventing runs. Pitchers that allow a batting average below 0.245 will typically allow less hits resulting in less runs allowed.
Batting Batting Average and Home Runs Players that have a high batting average do not always hit a lot of home runs as well as players that hit a lot of home runs do not always have a high batting average. Players with a high batting average but low number of home runs are considered as contact hitters. These hitters attempt to hit the ball in play to get on base rather than attempting to hit a home run. Players with a high number of home runs but a low batting average are considered as power hitters. These hitters attempt to hit home runs or for extra bases when at bat rather than trying to simply make contact. Players who have a high batting average and high number of home runs are considered to be excellent hitters.
Award Batting Champion and Batting Statistics Players that are recognized as the Batting Champion at the end of the season have the highest batting average in each league. These players always have at least a .300 batting average. These players typically have an on base percentage of .350 or higher and slugging percentage of .400 of .400 or higher. The players also usually have at least 85 runs created during the season (excluding the 2020 season).
Award Cy Young Award and Pitching Statistics The Cy Young Award is given to the best pitcher in each league. These pitchers typically have a WAR of 5.0 or higher, an ERA lower than 3.00, and at least 180 strikeouts (excluding the 2020 season). These pitchers also usally allow less than 175 hits during the season. These pitchers are valuable because they are consistently able to prevent opposing batters from scoring too many runs.
Pitching Earned Run Average, Strikeouts, Walks, and Errors Pitchers that have an ERA of 3.30 or lower typically have over 120 strikeouts, less than 60 walks, and less than 70 errors. These players typically strikeout at least 20% of the batters they face and make less mistakes when attempting to get an out. These pitchers are usually more effective at preventing runs from being scored by opposing teams.
Batting Extra Base Hit Percentage, On-Base Percentage, and Runs Created per Game Players that have an extra base hit percentage of 9.50% or higher typically have at least a .310 on-base percentage and at least 5 runs created per game. This can be due to their ability to get on base and into scoring position more often than others. This can be valuable as they are able to get their team more runs for a higher chance of winning.
Award Hank Aaron Award and Batting Statistics The Hank Aaron Award is given to the best hitter in each league. These players typically have a batting average of at least .280. They also usually have an on base percentage of at least .375, slugging percentage of at least .550, hit at least 35 home runs, and at least 100 RBIs (excluding the 2020 season). These players have great offensive performances throughout the season and contribute a lot of runs for their teams.
Batting Home Runs, Strikeouts, and Strikeout Percentage Players that have at least 25 home runs and 130 strikeouts typically have a strikeout percentage of at least 20.0%. These players typically swing for power rather than swinging for contact. Players that have at least 25 home runs and less than 130 strikeouts typically swing for contact and have strikeout percentage lower than 20.0%.
Award MVP Award and Player Statistics The Most Valuable Player Award is given to the best all-around player in each league. These players usually makes large contributions to their team offensively and defensively. These players typically have at least 6.0 Wins Above Replacement, a batting average of at least .300 (for non pitchers), an on base percentage of at least .370, and a slugging percentage of at least .550. The pitchers who have recieved the Most Valuable Player Award typically have an ERA of 3.20 or lower, at least 120 strikeouts. A good rule of thumb for players who may potentially win the Most Valuable Player Award is having a WAR of 6.0 or higher.
Team Payroll and Season Outcome A team's season outcome can be directly related to their payroll. In the past few seasons (excluding the 2020 season), 45 out of 54 teams who made it into postseason contention or farther typically had a payroll of at least $90,000,000. Teams that made it to the World Series typically had a payroll of at least $110,000,000. However, this is not always true for all teams as there are many teams with payrolls above $90,000,000 that were eliminated from postseason contention. There are 28 teams that had a payroll of at least $150,000,000 that were eliminated from postseason contention. This could be due to other factors not considered such as trades and injuries.
Team Payroll and Wins The number of wins that a team gets during a season can be directly related to their payroll. In the past few seasons (excluding the 2020 season), 55 out of 63 teams with 85 wins or more typically had a payroll of at least $75,000,000. 19 out of 27 teams with 95 wins or more typically had a payroll of at least $100,000,000. There are several teams who have payrolls above $75,000,000 that had less than 85 wins in a season that could be due to other factors such as trades and injuries.
Award Pitching ERA Champion and Pitching Statistics Players that are recognized as the Pitching ERA Champion at the end of the season have the lowest ERA in each league. These pitchers typically have an ERA of 2.75 or lower, at least 160 strikeouts, and less than 160 hits allowed. These players are excellent at preventing the opposing team from scoring runs.
Pitching Wins Above Replacement and Situational Wins Pitchers with at least 1.5 situational wins typically have 2.5 wins above replacement or higher. This means that these pitchers are more valuable than other average pitchers and are more successful in tough situations. These situations can include closing out an inning with the bases loaded or striking out a power hitter with a runner in scoring position.
Batting Walks and Walk Percentage Players that have at least 90 walks typically have a walk percentage of 12.50% or higher. These players can be seen as having more plate discipline than others. This allows them to get on base more often off of balls than other batters who may swing at balls more often.
Batting Wins Above Replacement and Win Probability Added by Offensive Player Players that have a WAR of 2.5 or higher typically have a Win Probability Added of at least 1.5. This means that these players regularly play well enough to increase their team's chances of winning. These players contribute a lot to their team's success whihc could include getting on base more often than other batters and scoring more runs. A good rule of thumb for WAR is that good players typically have a WAR of 3.0 or higher with potential Most Valuable Players having a WAR above 6.0. A good rule of thumb for Win Probability Added is that above average players have a WPA of 2.0 or higher.
Pitching Wins Above Replacement and Win Probability Added by Pitcher Players that have a WAR of 2.0 or higher typically have a Win Probability Added of at least 1.5. This means that these pitchers regularly play well enough to increase their team's chances of winning. These players contribute a lot to their team's success which could include reducing the amount of runs that opposing teams score. A good rule of thumb for WAR is that solid players typically have a WAR of 2.0 or higher with potential Most Valuable Players having a WAR above 6.0. A good rule of thumb for Win Probability Added is that above average players have a WPA of 2.0 or higher.
